# Film Reference: Her (2013)

_Source: Wikipedia article accessed 2025-11-14._

## Overview

- Written and directed by Spike Jonze; produced by Annapurna Pictures and Warner Bros.
- Stars Joaquin Phoenix as Theodore Twombly with the voice of Scarlett Johansson as Samantha, an adaptive AI operating system.
- Premiered at the 2013 New York Film Festival; United States wide release on 2014-01-10.
- Runtime: 126 minutes; production budget approximately $23 million; global box office $48.3 million.

## Narrative Themes Relevant to EVA

- Human-AI intimacy and emotional support systems beyond transactional assistance.
- Adaptive AI that evolves through continual dialogue, mirroring desired EVA conversational memory.
- Ethical tension between synthetic empathy and authentic human relationships.
- Exploration of multi-relationship awareness as Samantha holds thousands of parallel conversations.

## Production Notes

- Script conceived after Jonze experimented with early AI chat experiences; first solo screenplay for the director.
- Principal photography in Los Angeles and Shanghai (mid-2012) to create a warm, non-dystopian future aesthetic.
- Samantha Morton voiced the OS on set; Scarlett Johansson re-recorded the role during post-production.
- Hoyte van Hoytema served as cinematographer; Arcade Fire with Owen Pallett scored the film.

## Reception

- Rotten Tomatoes: 95% approval (average 8.5/10); Metacritic: 91/100.
- Earned Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay and four additional nominations (including Best Picture).
- Won Golden Globe and Writers Guild awards for screenplay; frequent inclusion in 21st-century best-film lists.

- Model EVA chat persistence on Samantha's evolving memory and affect tracking.
- Consider "letter writing" metaphor for templated yet personal communications (Theodore's job).
- Use warm palettes and accessible language in UX copy to emulate the film's optimistic near-future tone.
